How payment gateway works: simplified process for secure transactions

Step-by-step breakdown :

Step What Happens?
1.

Select product: Customer picks desired products/services for purchase.

2.

Payment page: Customers enter card details on the payment page (card number, name, expiry, CVV).

3.

Payment gateway: Encrypted card info reaches the Gateway, which conducts fraud checks before transmitting it to the acquirer.

4.

Fraud checks: The acquirer forwards data to card schemes for additional fraud checks before sending it to the issuing bank.

5.

Payment authorization: Issuing bank verifies transaction data, ensuring funds or a valid account before approval/decline.

6.

Payment message: Issuing bank informs acquirer (approved/declined); message relayed to the payment gateway.

7.

Payment confirmation: The payment gateway notifies the merchant, who displays confirmation to the customer or suggests alternatives if declined.

8.

Capture payment amount: After approval, the merchant 'captures' funds from the buyer's account to their merchant account; no billing until capture.

9.

Payment settlement: The acquirer secures funds from the issuing bank, 'holding' them in the merchant account, with settlement duration based on the merchant-provider agreement.
