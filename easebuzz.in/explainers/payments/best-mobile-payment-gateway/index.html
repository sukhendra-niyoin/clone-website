What is mobile payment gateway?

Mobile payment gateways connect your online store and customers’ bank accounts or cards. They enable safe and efficient payment processing on your website or app. With these gateways, customers can easily pay for their purchases through the app, while sellers can smoothly accept online payments.

How mobile payment gateway works: A simple explanation

Mobile payment gateways are a bridge between customers and businesses when making purchases through mobile apps, much like a card reader and POS system in a physical store.

Here’s how the process unfolds:

  1.    Customers add items to their cart and proceed to the checkout within the mobile app.
  2.    Customers select their preferred payment method at checkout, enter card details, or choose an alternative payment method.
  3.    The in-app payment gateway sends this information to the issuing bank, which may involve tokenization for added security.
  4.    The issuing bank verifies the transaction, checking if the customer’s account or credit card has enough funds.
  5.    Confirmation is sent back to the app via the payment gateway if the payment is approved.
  6.    Once settled, the payment moves to the business’s merchant account.

Choosing the right mobile payment gateway provider: What to look for?

Many mobile payment gateway providers exist, but not all are the same. When selecting one, consider these key factors:

  • Seamless checkout across devices : Choose a gateway compatible with various mobile devices and screen sizes to ensure a smooth payment experience for customers on both Android and iOS.

  • Security-certified checkout : Opt for a gateway that complies with PCI standards to enhance security and build customer trust. Trust badges indicate a safe merchant.

  • Multiple payment options : Offer diverse payment choices, including mobile and e-wallets like Google Pay, alongside traditional methods like credit cards. Cater to customer preferences.

  • Easy integration with your app : Pick a gateway with an SDK (Software Development Kit) for easy app integration. This minimizes developer involvement and maintains app speed.

Finding and selecting the right mobile payment provider

Once you know your business needs, consider these factors when choosing a provider:

  • Cost : Understand the pricing model, which may involve monthly fees, transaction charges, and extra costs for specific card types.

  • Transaction flow : Different providers have varying payment processes. Choose based on customer familiarity and comfort level with the process.

  • Merchant account : Check if the provider offers a merchant account or if you need to provide your own. Consider timeframes for money transfers in this decision.
